# Fact Checklist

Use this checklist before finalizing `content.md`.

## 1) Time and Event Consistency

- Verify years and sequence of historical events.
- Avoid mixing policy release date with implementation/result date.
- Keep terms consistent across slides.

## 2) Data Usage Rules

- Prefer publicly known and stable indicators.
- Keep the same metric definition across comparison points.
- Avoid exact numbers when confidence is low; use bounded wording.
- Do not fabricate sources or institutions.

## 3) Claim Strength Control

- Match claim strength to evidence quality.
- Use cautious wording for uncertain statements:
  - "generally"
  - "from available public data"
  - "can be observed in"
- Avoid absolute wording without strong evidence.

## 4) Theory-Evidence Balance

- Each key theoretical point should have at least one supporting example, case, or data point.
- Do not fill slides with abstract concepts only.
- Do not list data without explaining why it supports the argument.

## 5) Final Self-Check

- Can each slide be explained in 30-60 seconds?
- Does each speaker-note block directly correspond to the visible text?
- Is the ending slide a conclusion of the full line, not a disconnected slogan?
